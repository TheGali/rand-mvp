## Example 1: Roof — Membrane Deterioration (with engineer-stated cost)

**Field Notes (SPORT):**
```
S: Roof membrane, main roof level 12
P: Membrane showing extensive blistering and ridging, multiple patches visible from previous repairs
O: EPDM membrane approx 20 yrs old, blisters up to 12" diameter, base flashings pulling away at parapet
R: Full membrane replacement with new TPO system, replace base flashings and sheet metal
T: Year 1-2 (Immediate)
Cost: $150,000-$200,000
```

**RAND Prose:**
The existing EPDM roof membrane at the main roof level was observed to be in poor condition. The membrane, estimated to be approximately twenty years old, exhibits extensive blistering with individual blisters measuring up to twelve inches in diameter. Ridging was noted throughout, and evidence of multiple prior patch repairs was visible. Base flashings at the parapet walls were observed to be separating from the substrate. It is recommended that the existing membrane be removed and replaced with a new thermoplastic polyolefin (TPO) single-ply roof system. All base flashings and associated sheet metal should be replaced concurrently.

**Caption:** Photo 1-1: Main roof level — EPDM membrane blistering and ridging at main roof area

## Example 2: Exterior Walls — Brick Spalling (no cost stated)

**Field Notes (SPORT):**
```
S: East facade, floors 8-10
P: Brick spalling and mortar deterioration, steel lintel corrosion at window heads
O: Approx 200 SF affected area, lintels rusting and jacking bricks, some loose bricks noted
R: Scaffold east facade, replace deteriorated brick and mortar, clean and paint lintels, install through-wall flashing where missing
T: Year 1-2 (Immediate)
```

**RAND Prose:**
The east facade between the eighth and tenth floors was observed to exhibit brick spalling and mortar joint deterioration over an area of approximately two hundred square feet. Steel lintels at window head locations were found to be corroded, with expansion of the corroding steel displacing the brick masonry above. Loose brick units were noted at several locations. It is recommended that scaffolding be erected at the east facade to facilitate removal and replacement of all deteriorated brick masonry and repointing of deficient mortar joints. Corroded steel lintels should be cleaned to bare metal and coated with a rust-inhibitive primer and finish coat. Through-wall flashing should be installed at all locations where it is currently absent.

## Example 3: Plumbing — Riser Corrosion

**Field Notes (SPORT):**
```
S: Domestic water risers, basement mechanical room
P: Galv steel risers showing heavy corrosion and pinhole leaks, clamp repairs visible
O: Original 1962 galvanized risers, multiple clamp repairs, reduced flow reported on upper floors
R: Replace all galvanized domestic water risers with Type L copper
T: Year 1-3 (Short Term)
```

**RAND Prose:**
The domestic water risers, located in the basement mechanical room, were observed to be original galvanized steel piping dating to the building's 1962 construction. The risers exhibit heavy external corrosion and evidence of pinhole leak repairs utilizing hose clamps at multiple locations. Building management reported reduced water flow at upper-floor fixtures, consistent with internal corrosion and tuberculation of the galvanized piping. It is recommended that all galvanized domestic water risers be replaced with new Type L copper piping throughout.

**Caption:** Photo 5-1: Basement mechanical room — corroded galvanized domestic water risers with clamp repairs

## Example 4: Elevators — Modernization

**Field Notes (SPORT):**
```
S: Passenger elevators 1 and 2
P: Frequent breakdowns, long wait times, obsolete relay controllers
O: Original 1965 Otis geared traction machines, relay logic controls, manual doors on cab, slow door operators
R: Full modernization - new controllers, door operators, cab interiors, fixtures, keep existing machines
T: Year 3-5 (Medium Term)
```

**RAND Prose:**
Passenger elevators number one and number two were observed to be equipped with their original 1965 Otis geared traction machines and relay-based logic controllers. The elevator system was reported to experience frequent service interruptions and extended wait times during peak periods. Door operators were observed to be slow in operation and the cab interiors show significant wear. It is recommended that both passenger elevators undergo full modernization, including installation of new microprocessor-based controllers, new door operators, new cab interiors, and new hall and car fixtures. The existing traction machines, which appear to be in serviceable condition, may be retained.

**Caption:** Photo 8-1: Elevator machine room — original 1965 Otis geared traction machines and relay controllers

## Example 5: Interior — Lobby (abbreviated field notes)

**Field Notes (SPORT):**
```
lobby floor cracked tiles, walls need paint, ceiling water stain near entrance, mailboxes dented
recommend retile lobby floor, paint, replace damaged ceiling tiles, new mailboxes
probably year 1-2
```

**RAND Prose:**
The main lobby was observed to be in fair condition overall. The floor finish exhibits cracked and broken ceramic tiles at several locations. Wall surfaces show general wear and are in need of repainting. Water-stained ceiling tiles were noted in the vicinity of the main entrance, suggesting a possible leak from above. The tenant mailbox installation was observed to have multiple damaged and dented units. It is recommended that the lobby floor be retiled, wall surfaces repainted, damaged ceiling tiles replaced, and the mailbox installation replaced with a new unit. The source of the ceiling water stain should be investigated and corrected prior to ceiling tile replacement.

**Caption:** Photo 12-1: Main lobby — cracked floor tiles, water-stained ceiling, and damaged mailboxes
